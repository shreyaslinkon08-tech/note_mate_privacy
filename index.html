1. Plain-language summary

Note Mate is designed for private note-taking, memories, reminders, study workflows, sticky note widgets, AI writing help, exports, optional collaboration, and optional backup. Most day-to-day note editing happens locally on your device. Cloud services are used only for features that require them, such as sign-in, sync, collaboration, AI requests, push notifications, analytics, ads, remote configuration, and Google Drive backup.

Important privacy commitments: we do not sell your note content; we do not intentionally publish your private notes; we request Android permissions only for app features that need them; and cloud backup content is intended to be encrypted before upload where the encrypted backup flow is used.

Some information must still be processed by trusted service providers so the app can function. For example, Firebase may process account identifiers, device tokens, analytics events, crash or diagnostic data, remote configuration, cloud functions, and collaboration metadata. AI providers may process the note text or prompt you choose to send for an AI task. Google Drive processes backup files if you use Drive backup. Google AdMob may process advertising and device information if ads are enabled in your region and app build.

2. Scope and who this policy applies to

This policy applies to users of Note Mate on Android, including users in the United States, the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, India, and other regions. It applies when you use the app, create notes or memories, enable cloud features, use Mate AI, import or export files, use widgets, create reminders, collaborate with another user, contact support, or interact with notifications.

This policy does not replace privacy notices from third-party services that you independently use, such as Google Play, Google Drive, Firebase, Google AdMob, AI providers, your device manufacturer, your keyboard app, your launcher, WhatsApp or other sharing targets, or cloud storage providers. Those services may process information under their own privacy policies when you interact with them.

6. Local device storage, cloud storage, and encryption

Local device storage

Note Mate stores much of your active app data locally in the Android app sandbox, including notes, memories, sticky notes, media references, PDFs, app settings, reminder state, and cached data. Local device storage allows the app to remain fast and useful offline. Android's app sandbox helps isolate this data from other apps, but local device data may be accessible to someone who has access to your unlocked device, device backups outside Note Mate, rooted devices, compromised devices, debugging tools, or malware.

Cloud and backup storage

If you enable cloud backup, restore, collaboration, AI, messaging, or sign-in features, selected data may be sent to cloud services. Cloud backup and restore data is intended to be encrypted before upload in the encrypted backup flow. Some operational metadata may remain visible to service providers so the feature can work, such as file names, file IDs, timestamps, account IDs, collaborator IDs, app version, backup status, and sync metadata.

PIN/password warning: if a backup is protected by a PIN or password and you lose it, the encrypted backup content may be unrecoverable. This is intentional security behavior. You are responsible for remembering your backup PIN or password.

Encryption limits

Encryption protects data in many risk scenarios, but it is not magic. Data can still be exposed if your device is compromised, if you choose a weak PIN or password, if you share exported files, if a collaborator receives a decrypted copy, if you send content to an AI provider, if you paste content into another app, or if a third-party service processes metadata necessary to deliver the feature.

7. Mate AI assistant

Mate AI is an optional assistant designed to help with note-related tasks such as summarizing, correcting grammar, continuing writing, rewriting in a different style, generating study material, creating tags, improving structure, and answering questions about the current note. AI features may use backend functions and third-party AI providers such as Groq or OpenRouter, depending on configuration, availability, rate limits, and user-selected model options.

When you use Mate AI, the text you submit, your command, selected note context, selected model, and related technical metadata may be sent to backend infrastructure and AI providers to generate a response. Do not send sensitive information to AI unless you are comfortable with it being processed by those providers. AI responses may be inaccurate, incomplete, or inappropriate; you should review AI output before relying on it or saving it.

Note Mate should not automatically apply AI edits without your choice where preview/accept workflows are available. AI content may be temporarily processed for the request and may be subject to provider security, retention, abuse monitoring, or logging practices. We do not use Mate AI to intentionally create long-term personal memory about you outside the app's explicit saved content.

8. Backup, restore, and Google Drive

Note Mate may offer backup to Google Drive or other configured cloud storage. If you connect Google Drive, Google may process your account identity, authorization tokens, Drive file metadata, and backup files under Google's applicable terms and privacy policies. Note Mate uses Drive access to create, update, list, download, and restore app backup files needed for your backup workflow.

Backup can include notes, rich text state, media attachments, memories, PDFs, collections, reminders, tags, metadata, and app-specific export data. Restore may overwrite, merge, or import data depending on the feature flow. If restore fails because of a wrong PIN/password, missing files, revoked Drive permission, network interruption, or corrupted backup, Note Mate may be unable to recover the affected encrypted content.

You can revoke Google Drive or Google account access through your Google account settings. Revoking access may disable future backup or restore until you reconnect. Deleting local app data does not necessarily delete backup files already stored in Drive; you may need to delete them from Drive or use in-app deletion features where provided.

9. Collaboration and shared notes

Collaboration is optional. When you share a note with another registered user, Note Mate may process the owner's user ID, collaborator user IDs, collaborator email addresses, role assignments, note IDs, update timestamps, updated-by fields, and content or encrypted content depending on the collaboration implementation. The owner controls adding and removing collaborators and changing roles where the app supports it.

Collaborators may be able to view or edit shared note content according to their role. If a collaborator can view decrypted content, that collaborator may copy, screenshot, export, or share it outside Note Mate. Note Mate cannot guarantee confidentiality after content is intentionally shared with another person.

Collaboration security is enforced through server-side rules and authentication, but no system is perfect. If you believe a note was shared incorrectly, remove the collaborator, change relevant account credentials, and contact support.

11. Imports, exports, sharing, and `.links` files

Note Mate can import or export notes and files, including PDF, TXT, DOCX, images, audio, and Note Mate `.links` files. When you export or share a file, the receiving app, operating system share sheet, messaging app, cloud service, or recipient may process that file under their own rules. For example, if you share a `.links` file through WhatsApp, WhatsApp and the recipient may receive the file and related metadata.

`.links` files are intended as portable Note Mate files and may include encrypted or packaged note data depending on export settings. If you protect an exported file with a PIN/password, recipients may need that PIN/password to import it. If you export unencrypted or decrypted content, anyone with access to that file may read it.

Deleting a note from Note Mate may not delete copies you exported, shared, backed up to third-party services, sent to collaborators, or saved outside the app sandbox. You are responsible for managing files after they leave Note Mate.

We use technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the nature of the app, including Android app sandboxing, HTTPS-only network configuration, Firebase Authentication, Firebase security rules, App Check or Play Integrity where configured, release build shrinking and obfuscation, encrypted backup flows where supported, Android Keystore-backed local encryption for selected sensitive values, permission prompts, access controls, and abuse prevention.

The app may detect rooted devices, emulators, debugging, app tampering, outdated app versions, or integrity failures. 15. Retention and deletion

We keep personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. Retention periods vary depending on the type of data and feature.

  • Local notes, memories, widgets, attachments, and settings remain on your device until you delete them, clear app data, uninstall the app, or an app feature removes them.
  • Recycle bin items may remain until permanently deleted or until an automatic cleanup rule applies.
  • Cloud backup files remain in your connected cloud account until deleted through Note Mate, the cloud provider, or your account tools.
  • Firebase account, collaboration, messaging, analytics, and diagnostic records may remain for operational, security, fraud-prevention, legal, or backup reasons.
  • AI request logs or provider-side processing records may be retained by providers according to their policies and our configuration.
  • Support communications may be retained while needed to resolve your request and maintain records.

If you request account deletion, we will take reasonable steps to delete or de-identify personal information associated with your account, subject to legal, security, backup, fraud-prevention, and technical limitations. Deletion from active systems may not immediately remove data from encrypted backups, logs, collaborator copies, exported files, or third-party services you control.
