1. Plain-language summary

Note Mate is a productivity app for private notes, rich text, tasks, sticky notes, memories, study workflows, PDFs, reminders, widgets, optional AI help, optional collaboration, and optional cloud backup. Many core features work locally on your device. Online features use service providers only when needed for that feature.

Your notes are not sold.

We do not sell private note content. We do not intentionally publish private notes.

Cloud features are optional.

Sign-in, backup, restore, collaboration, AI, ads, analytics, and notifications may involve third-party services.

You control many choices.

You can deny Android permissions, avoid optional features, delete notes, export files, remove collaborators, and contact us for account requests.

Important: if you use Mate AI, collaboration, Google Drive backup, sharing, or exports, the information you choose to send or share can leave your device and may be processed by service providers or recipients under their own terms.

2. Scope and relationship to other services

This policy applies to Note Mate for Android and related services operated for the app. It applies when you install or use the app, create or import content, sign in, use Mate AI, use cloud backup, share or collaborate on notes, enable reminders, use widgets, contact support, receive notifications, or interact with ads or analytics.

This policy does not replace the privacy policies of third parties you independently use, such as Google Play, Google Drive, Firebase, Google AdMob, Groq, OpenRouter, your Android device manufacturer, your keyboard, your cloud storage provider, messaging apps, file managers, browser apps, or recipients you share content with.

6. Local storage, cloud storage, and encryption

Local device storage

Much of Note Mate's active data is stored locally in the Android app sandbox, including notes, sticky notes, memories, PDF references, settings, reminder state, and cached information. Android's app sandbox helps isolate this data from other apps, but data on your device may still be exposed if your device is unlocked, rooted, compromised, backed up outside Note Mate, accessed through debugging tools, or infected with malware.

Cloud storage and online processing

Cloud features may use Firebase, Google Drive, Google Play services, AI providers, and other service providers. Data may be uploaded or processed when you sign in, enable backup, restore from Drive, collaborate, receive push notifications, use Mate AI, use ads, or rely on remote configuration.

Encryption and passwords

Note Mate may use Android Keystore-backed protection, encrypted backup flows, protected `.links` exports, HTTPS, Firebase Authentication, App Check or Play Integrity, and access rules. Encryption availability can vary by feature. If you protect an export or backup with a PIN/password, losing that PIN/password may make encrypted content unrecoverable.

7. Mate AI assistant

Mate AI is optional. It can help summarize, rewrite, continue, correct, tag, explain, structure, and study from note content. AI features may use backend functions and third-party AI providers such as Groq or OpenRouter depending on availability, configuration, user settings, rate limits, and selected models.

When you use Mate AI, the prompt, selected command, selected model, note text or context you provide, generated output, and technical metadata may be sent to backend services and AI providers. You should not submit highly sensitive information to AI unless you are comfortable with that processing. AI output may be inaccurate, incomplete, offensive, or unsuitable; review output before relying on it.

AI choice: Note Mate is designed so AI actions happen when you initiate them. Avoid using AI on content you would not want processed by a third-party AI service.

8. Backup, restore, and Google Drive

If you connect Google Drive, Google may process your account identity, authorization tokens, Drive metadata, file IDs, and backup files under Google's terms and privacy policy. Note Mate uses Drive access for app backup and restore workflows, such as creating, listing, updating, downloading, and deleting backup files.

Backups may include notes, tasks, folders, tags, memories, rich text state, media attachments, PDFs, settings, reminders, and metadata. Restore may import, merge, or overwrite data depending on the feature flow. Revoking Drive access may stop future backup or restore until you reconnect. Deleting local app data does not necessarily delete backups already stored in Drive.

9. Collaboration and shared notes

Collaboration is optional. When you share a note, Note Mate may process owner IDs, collaborator IDs, collaborator emails, note IDs, roles, invitations, timestamps, version or patch data, updated-by fields, conflict metadata, and shared content where applicable. Access controls are enforced through authentication and server-side rules.

Collaborators may view, edit, copy, export, screenshot, or further share content according to their role and what they can access. Once you intentionally share content with another person, Note Mate cannot guarantee what that person does with it outside the app.

11. Sharing, imports, exports, and `.links` files

Note Mate may import and export PDFs, TXT, DOCX, images, audio, and Note Mate `.links` files. When you share or export files, the receiving app, share sheet, cloud service, or recipient may process the file under their own policies. Deleting content from Note Mate may not delete exported files, Drive backups, collaborator copies, screenshots, or files saved outside the app sandbox.

`.links` files may contain packaged Note Mate data and may be protected with a PIN/password depending on export settings. If an exported file is unencrypted or decrypted, anyone with access to that file may read it.
